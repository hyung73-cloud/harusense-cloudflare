Full treatment path — first visit to maintenance

Obesity care covers starting, tapering, and stopping medication. Below is a typical flow from specialty clinics — reference only; your path follows your doctor’s plan.

  1. 01
    Initial

    First visit & assessment

    Review contraindications, conditions (diabetes, BP, thyroid, pancreas, etc.), meds, and family history. Height, weight, waist, BMI; labs when needed.

    Goal: what fits you now, not “one best drug.” Harusense helps compare cost later — it does not prescribe.

  2. 02
    Reduction

    Early weight-loss phase

    Appetite stabilizes; night eating and binges often ease; early weight response is tracked. Nausea, constipation, reflux — managed alongside meal rhythm.

    Early response shapes dose and maintenance later. Map prices help budget the first month/pen; packages and labs may be extra.

  3. 03
    Maintenance

    Maintenance — the core phase

    For many, keeping weight off is harder than losing it. Protect muscle; lock in new eating and movement habits.

    Protein, sleep, stress, strength + cardio matter here. Medication alone without habit change raises regain risk after stop.

  4. 04
    Plateau

    Plateau

    Weight flat for days or weeks is often not failure — the body finding a new set point.

    Revisit dose, meal order, sleep, strength work, stress; adjust with your doctor. How you handle plateau drives long-term outcome.

  5. 05
    Tapering

    Tapering

    When weight and appetite are stable, dose or interval is reduced gradually. Sudden stop is often discouraged.

    Oral add-ons or intermittent use vary by person. Build skills to manage without injections over time.

  6. 06
    After

    After treatment ends

    Self-management continues after taper or stop. Regain? Discuss follow-up or restart with your clinic.

    Harusense stays useful for price and clinic lookup whenever you need it again.

Obesity is chronic. Like hypertension or diabetes, it needs ongoing check-ins and lifestyle work — visits should review weight, appetite, side effects, and habits each time, not become routine only.

Lifestyle changes — what to fix with the medication

GLP-1 drugs (Mounjaro, Wegovy, etc.) blunt appetite strongly, but medication alone is not enough. They buy time to build habits. Use that window for the steps below — key to maintenance and avoiding regain.

🥗 Eating

  1. Vegetables → protein → carbs order. Fiber first often smooths glucose and insulin after carbs.
  2. Slower gastric emptying: GLP-1 keeps you full longer. Practice stopping before “stuffed” — it helps after you taper off.
  3. Protein every meal to limit muscle loss in loss and maintenance phases.
  4. Night eating, alcohol, ultra-processed food — tackle habits that return quickly after stop.
  5. Short logs (photo, note, 3-day average weight) help you and your doctor.

🚶 Movement

  1. Pick something daily — stairs, walks, commute steps often beat “gym 3× only.”
  2. Zone 2 walking: can talk, slightly breathless (~60–70% max heart rate). Good for fat use and insulin sensitivity in many studies.
  3. 5–10 min strength before cardio — squats, lunges, push-ups, then walk or bike (adjust for joints).
  4. In maintenance, raise strength share — scale weight alone hides muscle loss.
  5. Aim for ~7 hours sleep; short sleep can worsen appetite and insulin resistance.

Visceral fat in Korea. Normal BMI with belly fat still risks prediabetes and fatty liver. Labs (fasting glucose, HbA1c, triglycerides, liver enzymes) matter. Zone 2 walks plus light strength often help visceral fat — set goals with your doctor.

Beyond the number on the scale

Less night snacking, easier stairs, better checkup numbers, managing weight without injections — that is real success.

Why treatment length differs

Appetite pattern, stress, sleep, binge tendency, comorbidities, and home/work life all change the timeline. Some need only 2–3 months of injections; others 6–12+ months of maintenance and taper. There is no single answer — plan it in clinic.

Many clinics plan 3–6 months loss + maintenance/taper. Rushing off medication without a solid maintenance phase often leads to regain.

Treatment & maintenance — common questions

Q. Can I stop as soon as I lose weight?
Usually you taper after weight and appetite stabilize. Abrupt stop is often discouraged; without habits, weight tends to return.
Q. Do I need injections forever?
No. Many aim for maintenance without drugs. With comorbidities, intermittent use is sometimes discussed.
Q. Will appetite return after stop?
Drug effect fades, but meal pace, portions, and order you practiced often remain — why tapering takes time.
Q. Can I lose weight without exercise?
Scale may drop, but muscle loss and regain risk is higher without light strength + walking. See lifestyle section above.
Q. Why is maintenance so important?
Holding a lower weight for a while resets body and eating norms. Skipping maintenance and stopping fast often leads to regain.
