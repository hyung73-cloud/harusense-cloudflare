What is listed on the map?

Wegovy (semaglutide) is a weekly injectable GLP-1 therapy used for obesity management in Korea under physician supervision. On Harusense you can filter by pen dose — typically 0.25mg, 0.5mg, 1.0mg, 1.7mg, 2.4mg — and compare pins in Mapo, Seodaemun, Yeongdeungpo, Gangseo, Yongsan, and nearby areas.

Prices are self-pay (non-insurance) and set by each clinic. The number on a clinic pin is not “drug only”: it usually reflects visit + medication bundle as reported by the clinic. If you only need a prescription to fill at a pharmacy, ask explicitly when you call.

Wegovy vs Mounjaro in Korea

Both are incretin-based therapies; neither is “always better.” Choice depends on medical history, side effects, dosing preference, and budget. Harusense does not rank clinics — we provide transparent price comparison. See also: Mounjaro price Korea.

FAQ — Wegovy in Korea

Is the map price final? No. Always confirm on the day of visit. Promotions, packages, and tests can change the bill.

Do clinics speak English? Some do; call first. This site is informational, not a booking service.

Can I get only a prescription? Many clinics can issue prescriptions when medically appropriate; policies differ.

Is Harusense a hospital? No. We publish price information with physician advisory input; treatment is between you and your clinic.
